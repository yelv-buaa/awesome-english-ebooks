Poems

Second Nature

By Paul Tran April 29, 2024

I lived
An invisible life.
Like wisteria

In winter,
I grew, broke
Through depths

Nobody fathomed.
I devoured wind,
Wicked light

God invented.
I invented drama.
Just because

Was reason
Enough. To hope
Against hope

I became
Terrible, terrifying, terrific—
By spring

There was
No stopping me.
I suffered

No fools,
Except my nature.
To display

My genius,
I carried on.
I transitioned

Every season.
I was seasoned.
How? Because

I’m now
Mystery and clarity:
The candle

Flame casting
On the wall
No shadow

Yet making
Everything around it
A shadow.
